code review

(sometimes referred to as peer review) is a software quality assurance activity in which one or several people check a program mainly by viewing and reading parts of its source code, and they do so after implementation or as an interruption of implementation. At least one of the persons must not be the code's author. The persons performing the checking, excluding the author, are called "reviewers". Keep reading

Why code review is critical

  • Ensure that you have no bugs in code.
  • Minimize your chances of having issues.
  • Confirm new code adheres to guidelines.
  • Increase the efficiency of new code.
